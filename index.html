About Curoria

Making academic research accessible, on both sides of the study

Curoria is a student research network designed to make academic research more accessible — helping researchers find participants, and helping volunteers contribute to discovery.

The core idea is simple: researchers should have a trusted place to share participant opportunities, and volunteers should have one organized place to understand and discover studies they may want to support.

Reviewed, not openStudy listings are checked before they are shown publicly.
Recognition, not pressureVolunteers build a contribution history while choosing studies freely.
Built for emerging researchersCuroria is designed to make research access easier for students and developing researchers.
One connected networkResearchers, volunteers, and supporting organizations contribute to the same research loop.

Overview

Curoria Research Standards

Curoria connects researchers who need participants with people who want to contribute to surveys and studies. These standards explain what a participant-facing listing must communicate before it can appear in the Curoria marketplace.

Be transparentExplain who is conducting the study, what participants will do, and how their information will be handled.
Recruit responsiblyUse accurate eligibility rules and avoid pressure, misleading promises, or hidden participation requirements.
Label review honestlyCuroria platform review is separate from institutional, ethics, or IRB approval and must never be presented as the same thing.
Keep listings accurateResearchers are responsible for correcting important changes to eligibility, links, procedures, privacy practices, or oversight status.
Recruitment

Responsible participant recruitment

Every Curoria listing should give a potential participant enough information to understand the opportunity before they choose whether to continue to the researcher's survey or study.

Every public listing should clearly state

PurposeA plain-language explanation of what the study is about without making unsupported claims.
ParticipationWhat participants will be asked to do, where participation happens, and the expected time commitment.
EligibilityAge range and any other meaningful requirements or exclusions that determine who can participate.
Researcher detailsThe responsible researcher or team, contact information, and relevant institution or supervisor when applicable.
CompensationWhether compensation or incentives are offered, including important conditions or limits.
Voluntary choiceParticipation must be presented as optional. Recruitment language should not pressure people or hide material conditions.
Minors

Age and participant protection

Research involving minors can require additional consent, permission, safeguarding, and institutional processes. Researchers must accurately state participant ages and follow every requirement that applies to their specific study.

For Curoria's initial public marketplace, the participant-recruitment pathway is designed for adults age 18 and older until a dedicated minor-consent and safeguarding system is implemented.

Before any future minor recruitment is enabled

Age must be accurateA listing cannot describe an adult-only study as open to younger participants or encourage anyone to misstate their age.
Required permissions must existResearchers are responsible for applicable parental or guardian permission, participant assent, institutional review, and safeguarding procedures.
Data collection must be appropriateStudies should minimize unnecessary personal information and clearly explain how information about younger participants is protected.
Curoria access is not approvalBeing able to create an account or submit a listing does not establish that a study involving minors is ethically or legally cleared.
Privacy

Privacy and data transparency

Participants should know what information a study may collect before they leave Curoria and enter an external survey or research system.

A listing should explain

What is collectedState whether the study requests names, contact information, demographics, recordings, sensitive responses, or other identifiable data.
Who can access itIdentify the researcher, research team, institution, or service providers that may have access when relevant.
Where participants go nextMake it clear when a participant will leave Curoria for an external survey, interview, scheduling, or research platform.
How long information is keptWhere practical, explain retention, deletion, withdrawal, or de-identification practices in the study's own consent materials.

Curoria's platform privacy practices and a researcher's study-specific data practices are separate. Researchers remain responsible for accurately describing their own study's handling of participant information.

Researcher Identity

Accurate researcher identity and responsibility

People deciding whether to participate should be able to understand who is responsible for a Curoria listing and how any school, supervisor, laboratory, or organization is actually connected to the study.

Use accurate identitiesResearchers should provide a real name or an accurately described research team identity together with current contact information.
Describe affiliations honestlyA school email, student club, faculty mentor, research lab, and formal institutional sponsorship are different relationships and should never be presented as interchangeable.
Name the responsible researcherEvery participant-facing listing should identify who can answer questions about the study and who is responsible for the information shown on Curoria.
Correct inaccurate claimsMaterial errors in researcher names, affiliations, supervision, verification, or oversight status should be corrected promptly.
AI Use

Responsible AI use in Curoria-listed research

Researchers may use appropriate AI and automated tools when planning or communicating their work, but they remain responsible for accuracy, participant privacy, research integrity, and every claim shown in a Curoria listing.

Never fabricate research activityAI must not be used to invent participants, responses, observations, results, approvals, affiliations, or researcher identities and present them as real.
Verify participant-facing informationResearchers should review AI-assisted study descriptions, eligibility language, consent explanations, code, analysis, and summaries before using them.
Protect participant informationConfidential or identifying participant information should not be entered into AI tools that are not approved for handling that data.
Follow the study's own rulesResearchers remain responsible for any institutional, course, sponsor, ethics-review, or platform requirements governing AI use in their study.
Enforcement

Curoria review and enforcement

Curoria may act when a listing is incomplete, misleading, unsafe, inaccurate, or inconsistent with platform standards. The goal is to protect the clarity and trustworthiness of the participant marketplace.

Possible review outcomes

ApproveThe listing may go live when the required participant-facing information is clear and complete.
Request changesCuroria may ask the researcher to clarify eligibility, procedures, privacy, oversight, or other material information before the listing goes live.
Pause or removeA live listing may be paused or removed when important information changes, a link becomes unsafe or misleading, or a serious standards concern is identified.
DeclineCuroria may decline listings that do not fit the platform, cannot be presented transparently, or create safety concerns the platform cannot responsibly support.
Curoria approval means only that a public listing passed Curoria's platform screening. It does not guarantee the scientific quality of a study and does not provide institutional ethics or IRB approval.

How Curoria Works

Curoria follows the same simple idea as the original platform: one network, three roles, one shared loop.

1. Researchers submit

Create an account, describe your study clearly, explain who can participate and what they will do, and submit the listing for review.

2. Curoria reviews

Curoria checks the public listing for clarity, completeness, transparency, and fit with platform standards before it appears in the research marketplace.

3. Volunteers discover and participate

People browse studies that interest them, review the eligibility and time commitment, and choose for themselves whether to continue to the researcher's study.

4. Contributions are tracked

Signed-in members can keep a history of the research opportunities they join, while researchers can keep track of their Curoria submissions.

5. Organizations support the loop

Schools, community groups, mentors, and partner organizations can help researchers and volunteers access responsible opportunities without changing Curoria's central purpose: connecting research with people.

Transparency & Trust

Curoria works best when participants can understand a study before they click into it and when researchers can describe their work without overstating approval or affiliation.

What “Curoria reviewed” means

It means Curoria checked the public listing against platform standards. It does not automatically mean a university, IRB, ethics committee, government agency, or other institution approved the study.

Researcher information

Names, institutional affiliations, school emails, supervisor involvement, and formal ethics-review status should be shown separately and only when Curoria has a reasonable basis to display them.

Participant information

Study cards and detail pages should make eligibility, expected time, study activities, researcher contact, privacy information, and any compensation easy to find.

External study links

Many studies may take place on an external survey or research platform. Curoria helps people discover the opportunity, but the researcher's study environment may have its own privacy terms and data practices.

Honest platform numbers

Curoria should display only real counts for studies, researchers, and participation activity. When a number is unknown or unavailable, it should not be replaced with an invented statistic.

Survey & Study Submission Guidelines

The main Curoria submission pathway is for researchers who need participants. A strong listing helps volunteers understand the opportunity before deciding whether to participate.

Tell people what the study is about

Use a clear title and plain-language description of the research question or purpose.

Explain what participation involves

State the activities, estimated time, eligibility requirements, number of participants needed, and any compensation.

Identify the researcher

Provide an accurate researcher name, contact email, relevant institution or supervision information, and the most accurate description of any ethics or institutional review.

Explain privacy and consent

Describe what information is collected, whether participants can stop, how consent is handled, and where the study itself is hosted.

After submission

Curoria may approve the public listing, request changes, ask for clarification, or decline to publish it. Curoria platform review must never be presented as formal ethics or IRB approval.

Curoria Study Listing Review

Curoria's core review process is designed for public study listings in the participant marketplace. It focuses on whether a listing is clear, transparent, and appropriate to publish on the platform.

1. Submission check

Curoria checks that required study, researcher, participant, contact, privacy, and oversight information is present.

2. Clarity review

The listing should explain the opportunity in language a potential participant can understand before leaving Curoria.

3. Trust and safety review

Curoria may flag unclear consent language, misleading approval claims, missing researcher information, unsuitable participant targeting, or other concerns that should be resolved before the listing goes live.

4. Decision

A listing may be approved, returned for changes, paused for clarification, or declined. Curoria listing review is a platform screening process and is not a substitute for formal institutional or ethics review.

5. Ongoing corrections

Researchers should update listings when recruitment closes or important study details change. Curoria may correct, pause, or remove listings when needed.

Privacy Policy

Last updated: July 19, 2026.

This policy should be reviewed before public launch so it accurately matches the services Curoria actually uses.

Information Curoria may collect

Curoria may collect account information, research submission details, study-joining activity connected to a user's dashboard, and information users provide through contact or application forms.

How Curoria uses information

Information may be used to operate accounts, review and display research listings, help users track their Curoria activity, communicate with researchers and participants, prevent abuse, and improve the platform.

Research conducted outside Curoria

When a participant follows a study link to an external survey or research system, that external service and the researcher may collect information under their own privacy practices. Curoria does not automatically control data collected inside those external systems.

Service providers

This site may use services such as Supabase for authentication and Netlify for hosting or form processing. Their privacy practices also apply to information they process.

Participant age and safeguarding

Curoria should only enable participant pathways that its consent, privacy, and safeguarding processes can responsibly support. Researchers remain responsible for requirements that apply to their own studies.

Corrections and deletion

Users may contact Curoria about corrections or deletion requests, subject to applicable legal, safety, and recordkeeping obligations.

Contact

Privacy questions may be sent to curoriafoundation@gmail.com.

Terms of Use

Last updated: July 19, 2026.

These starter terms should be reviewed before public launch and adapted to Curoria's actual legal structure.

What Curoria does

Curoria connects researchers who need participants with people who want to discover and contribute to research. It may also provide study results, educational challenges, community programs, and research-participation resources that support that same mission.

Researcher responsibilities

Researchers are responsible for accurate study information, ethics and consent requirements, privacy and data handling, participant safety, and compliance with applicable institutional or legal requirements.

Participant choice

Participation in research is voluntary. Users should review the study information and the researcher's own consent materials before taking part.

Platform review

Curoria may review, request changes to, pause, correct, or remove listings. Platform review does not guarantee research quality and does not substitute for formal institutional or ethics approval.

External study services

Curoria may link to third-party surveys, repositories, or research tools that Curoria does not control.

Supporting programs

Research results, educational challenges, and local Curoria communities are optional extensions of the central researcher-participant network.

Accessibility Statement

  • Keyboard-accessible navigation and controls.
  • Visible focus states.
  • Semantic headings and form labels.
  • Reduced-motion support.
  • Responsive layouts.
  • Color contrast designed for readability.
